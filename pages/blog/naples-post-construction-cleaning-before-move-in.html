Post-Construction Cleaning

Post-Construction Cleaning in Naples, FL: Remodel or New Build — What You Need Before Move-In

Sparklean Cleaning · Naples, Bonita Springs, Fort Myers, Estero & Cape Coral

Dust does not stop when the contractor packs up. After a kitchen remodel, bathroom renovation, or new construction home, fine debris hides in vents, inside cabinets, on window tracks, and across every horizontal surface. Standard house cleaning is not built for that layer of silica dust, drywall compound, adhesive residue, and trade footprints.

Sparklean’s post-construction cleaning is built for dust-heavy resets—not weekly tidy visits. Pair your scope with the right move-in maintenance plan once debris is under control, especially for seasonal properties in Estero or Naples. Tell us your closing or punch-list date and we will schedule the phases.

Post-Construction Cleaning vs. Regular House Cleaning

Regular residential cleaning maintains a lived-in home using standard products and equipment. Post-construction cleaning is a full reset of a jobsite into a finished interior — often requiring multi-pass dusting, HEPA focus areas, and careful detail work on new finishes like stone, engineered wood, and fresh paint.

Skipping a proper builder's clean means dust recirculates through your HVAC, settles on new paint, and shows up again after you unpack. In Naples, Bonita Springs, Fort Myers, Estero, and Cape Coral, coastal air and humidity can also make residues feel “sticky” faster.

Who Needs This Service

  • Your remodel just wrapped — cabinets are in, but every shelf has grit and there is film on tile and glass.
  • Your new build is nearing closing — you want a move-in ready clean before movers or staging.
  • Your GC did a rough clean — but you still see dust on baseboards, inside closets, and on light fixtures.
  • You are a homeowner, investor, or property manager with a turnover or punch-list date.

What Sparklean Covers on a Post-Construction Clean

  • Multi-pass dusting of walls, trim, ledges, doors, and door frames
  • Interior windows & tracks, removal of stickers and light residue
  • Cabinet interiors & exteriors, drawers, and closets
  • Kitchen & baths: sinks, counters, fixtures; careful treatment of new stone and grout
  • Floors: vacuuming and appropriate damp mopping for dust-heavy sites
  • Surface wipe-down of appliances, railings, and built-ins

Rough Clean vs. Final Clean

Many projects benefit from two phases. A rough clean after major trades exit reduces dust before flooring protection comes off. A final clean after last touch-ups delivers the “camera-ready” standard before the owner walkthrough.
