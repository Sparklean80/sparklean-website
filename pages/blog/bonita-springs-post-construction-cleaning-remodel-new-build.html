Remodel vs. New Construction in Bonita Springs

Kitchen and bath remodels in established golf-community homes leave grout haze, compound dust, and trade debris in lived-in spaces. New builds and major additions — common in Pelican Landing and Bonita Bay — need full builder’s cleans before furniture and art go in. Both require more than a standard maid visit.

What a Builder’s Clean Covers

  • Multi-pass dust removal on walls, trim, doors, and millwork
  • Interior windows, tracks, and label or sticker residue
  • Inside and outside of cabinets, closets, and built-ins
  • Careful treatment of new stone, tile, and freshly painted surfaces
  • HEPA vacuuming and damp mopping for silica and drywall particulate

Rough Clean vs. Final Clean

Many Bonita Springs projects need a rough clean after major trades finish, then a final clean after punch-list touch-ups — before your designer, photographer, or moving crew arrives. We coordinate timing with your GC or project manager.

Who Books Post-Construction Cleaning Here

  • Homeowners closing on a custom build in Bonita Bay or Pelican Landing
  • Investors preparing a flip or seasonal rental after renovation
  • Property managers after tenant-improvement work
  • Builders needing handoff-ready detail before owner walkthrough
